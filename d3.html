Idukki is a district located in the Indian state of Kerala, known for its picturesque beauty and rich biodiversity. The district is nestled in the Western Ghats mountain range and is famous for its lush green hills, forests, tea plantations, waterfalls, and wildlife. One of the main attractions of Idukki is the Idukki Dam, which is one of the largest arch dams in Asia. The dam is built on the Periyar River and is surrounded by scenic views of the hills and forests. The Idukki Wildlife Sanctuary, which is situated nearby, is home to a diverse range of wildlife such as elephants, tigers, leopards, deer, and various species of birds. Idukki is also known for its tea plantations, which are spread across the hills and valleys of the district. The tea gardens offer panoramic views of the surrounding hills and provide an opportunity to learn about the process of tea cultivation and processing. Another popular attraction in Idukki is the Munnar Hill Station, which is located in the northern part of the district. Munnar is known for its tea plantations, rolling hills, and stunning waterfalls. The hill station is a popular destination for trekking, camping, and wildlife safaris. Idukki is also home to a number of picturesque waterfalls, such as the Cheeyappara Waterfalls and the Keezharkuthu Falls. These waterfalls offer a refreshing break from the heat and humidity of the region and are a popular spot for picnics and photography. In terms of transportation, Idukki is well-connected by road and rail to other parts of Kerala and India. The nearest airports are the Cochin International Airport and the Madurai Airport, both of which are located at a distance of around 150 km from Idukki. Overall, Idukki is a nature lover's paradise, offering a unique blend of stunning landscapes, rich wildlife, and a peaceful ambiance. Whether you are interested in exploring the tea plantations, trekking through the hills, or simply enjoying the beauty of the waterfalls, Idukki has something for everyone.
